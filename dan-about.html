What is DAN?

The Decentralised Accountability Network (DAN) is a supportive space where members of 5 Creative 2026 can voluntarily commit to personal rules and habits, and hold each other accountable through mutual respect and shared responsibility.

Our Core Belief: Meaningful change comes from within, and social support strengthens personal commitment. We operate until SPM examinations, helping each other grow through equality, autonomy, and trust.

Unlike traditional organizations, DAN has no hierarchy. There are no presidents, secretaries, or authority figures. Every member has equal rights and responsibilities. We believe in the power of peer support, not top-down control.

Foundational Principles

Decentralization

DAN operates without administrative hierarchy. All members possess equal rights and responsibilities within the network.

Privacy Protection

Members are only asked for information directly relevant to their chosen commitments. Personal information is never collected or shared beyond this scope.

Personal Autonomy

All participation is entirely voluntary. Members freely choose their commitments, partners, and terms. No coercion, ever.

How It Works

The Simple Process

  1. Choose Your Commitment: Decide on a personal rule or habit you want to follow (e.g., study for 30 minutes daily, complete homework on time).
  2. Find a Partner: Connect with one or more classmates who want to support each other in reaching your goals.
  3. Create a Written Statement: Together, draft an agreement outlining your commitments, reporting frequency, and agreed-upon penalties from our approved list.
  4. Get a Witness (for pairs): If you're partnering with just one other person, have a third classmate sign as a witness for oversight.
  5. Support Each Other: Report your progress, celebrate wins, and help each other when things get tough.

Accountability Partnerships

What Makes a Good Partnership?

Every partnership in DAN must be:

  • Nurturing - supportive and encouraging, never punitive
  • Voluntary - freely entered and freely exited
  • Sustainable - realistic and maintainable over time

When Your Partner Struggles

Supporting each other is at the heart of DAN. When a partner has difficulty meeting their commitment, you should:

  1. Ask if the rule is too demanding right now
  2. Discuss together to find better approaches
  3. Seek advice from witnesses or other members if needed
  4. Always prioritize their wellbeing over rigid enforcement

Dissolving a Partnership

Sometimes partnerships need to end, and that's okay. You can dissolve a partnership if:

  • At least one person feels it's no longer nurturing, AND
  • You've attempted at least once to resolve issues

No further justification is required. This protects everyone's autonomy and wellbeing.

Approved Penalties

Penalties exist to support accountability, not to punish. They should be meaningful but respectful of dignity and wellbeing.

Social/Reciprocal

  • Buy partner a drink/snack
  • Treat partner to a meal
  • Write a thank-you note
  • Small gift (max RM10)

Service-Based

  • Help with homework/revision (30 min)
  • Perform a reasonable favor
  • Share study notes/resources

Self-Improvement

  • Additional study session (+15 min)
  • Written reflection on the missed goal
  • Complete missed task twice next day

Financial

  • Class fund contribution (RM2-5)
  • Donation to agreed cause

Creative/Encouragement

  • Create motivational message/poster
  • Other creative tasks (by agreement)
Strictly Prohibited:
  • Anything humiliating, degrading, or shaming
  • Financial penalties exceeding RM10 or causing hardship
  • Violations of school rules
  • Physical penalties of any kind
  • Public humiliation or embarrassment

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can join DAN?

DAN is open to all members of 5 Creative 2026.

Can I leave if I change my mind?

Absolutely. You can leave DAN or dissolve any partnership at any time without penalty. Your participation is completely voluntary.

What if my partner isn't being honest about their reports?

DAN operates on trust and unilateral reporting. Partners don't investigate each other. If you feel the partnership isn't working, you can discuss it with your partner or dissolve the partnership.

What if I'm struggling with my commitment?

That's exactly when your partner can help most! They should ask if the commitment is too demanding, help you find better approaches, and prioritize your wellbeing. The goal is growth, not perfection.

Can I have multiple partnerships?

Yes! You can form as many accountability partnerships as feel sustainable for you.

What's the role of a witness?

Witnesses sign Written Statements for two-person partnerships to provide oversight and prevent abuse. They can also serve as mediators if both partners request help resolving conflicts.

How do we amend the Charter?

Any member can propose amendments. Depending on what's being changed, different levels of consensus are required (75%, 90%, or 100%). Core principles require 100% agreement to change.

When does DAN end?

DAN operates until the completion of SPM examinations, after which it automatically dissolves. Individual partnerships can continue if partners mutually agree.
